Rogue

Not everyone needs magic or big weapons to be dangerous, some people use their razor sharp wits and movements to make due in this hostile world. People that have this class have access to more talents than the other classes, making them a versatile unit that can fit into any mold. Be it Court intrigue, a heist or a careful raid, a rogue is always the first choice.

Primary stats: Accuracy, Dexterity

Weapon groups: Brawling, Light Blades and either Blackpowder or Crossbows

Archetypes

Ranger

Quick reflexes and a keen eye work hand in hand with a bow. Rogues from this background work best from a distance, making sure to hit whatever target they have in sight. Good positioning allows them to control the battlefield, while remaining safe.

Primary stats: Perception, Willpower

Weapon groups: Two from Bows, Crossbows, Slings, Pistols, Thrown, Staves

Scout

While some rogues work from the shadows, others join the fight. These rogues are best at moving quickly through the battlefield, they are always on edge and quite perceptive to their surroundings. Best used by those who want to act ahead and cut their enemies before their plans take place.

Primary stats: Fighting, Willpower

Weapon groups: Two from Chains, Flails, Spears, Staves

Shiv

Fair combat may not be something that everyone likes, especially not the Shives, staying in the shadows, striking unaware targets with a deadly blow. These people are mostly assassins or thugs, but also subtle nobles.

Primary stats: Communication, Perception

Weapon groups: Curved Blades or Chains

Archetype Features

Barbarian

  • Offence is the best Defence: General archetype perk, passive, if you wear Light Armor or Clothing (Or nothing, I don’t judge), you get +1 to defence and speed.
  • Power Strike: when you use the All-Out Attack Action, you deal a +3 bonus to damage instead of the base +1, but you get a -2 to def instead of the base -1.
  • Heavy Hit: twice per encounter, you may take a -2 on an attack roll to gain a damage bonus equal to your Constitution score.
  • Daring Charge: Whenever you make a Charge Action, you gain a +2 bonus on the attack instead of a +1 and you add your Might Focus to the damage if you hit.
  • Chopping Block: Immediately after you kill a foe in combat, you can make another attack against a different foe in range as a free action. If no foe is in range, you may instead perform a free Move action to put you in range of a new target. If you kill your foe with the free attack, you do not get another free attack or move.

Fighter

graph TD E{{Weapons At Hand}} A["Quick Draw (Lvl. 7)"] B["Veteran (Lvl. 7)"] C["Quick Strike (Lvl. 13)"] D["Fight With Style (Lvl. 13)"] A & B --> C & D classDef green fill:#add3ae, color:#fff; class E green
  • Weapons At Hand: All penalties for using weapons from a Weapons Group you do not have is reduced to a -2 and you suffer no reduction in damage.
  • Quick Draw: Your training with multiple weapons has left you with some quick reflexes. Once per turn, you may switch your weapons as a free action.
  • Veteran: Your experience with specific types of weapons had placed you one step above the enemies. You may also add your weapon focus bonus to the damage you deal with each attack.
  • Quick Strike: You can make a Melee Attack vs. an adjacent opponent as a minor action. The attack roll and damage of the Quick Strike suffer a –2 penalty.
  • Fight With Style: When using any Style, all your attacks gain a +1 bonus in addition to what the Style may give you.

Knight

  • Teamwork: When you use the Set Up Stunt on two allies instead of just one.
  • Precise Strike: When you use the Aim Action on a melee attack, you gain a bonus to damage as much as you do in attack.
  • Challenge: Select one target to Challenge as a minor action. So long as you focus on them, you gain a +1 attack and damage bonus against that target. You can only have one selected target at a time.
  • Expert Strike: When you make an attack, you can take up to a –3 penalty on your attack roll to receive a corresponding bonus to your damage roll.You must choose before you roll.
  • Call To Arms: You gain access to the Call to Arms Combat stunt, (3-5 SP), when you use this stunt, you may command a willing creature to take action on your turn, for 3 stunts, they gain a minor action, from 5 stunts, they gain a major action. Their action does not generate SP.
